Protecting Yourself: Fraud Prevention

Article

Financial fraud is when someone tries to trick you into giving them your money or personal information. Scammers often pretend to be from banks, government offices, or even family members. They might call, send messages, or come to your home.

Common fraud types include:

  • Phishing: Getting fake messages or calls asking for bank details, PINs, or OTPs.
  • OTP Fraud: Someone asking for your OTP to complete a transaction they are doing. Never share OTPs unless you initiated the transaction.
  • Job Scams/Lottery Scams: Promising a job or lottery win if you pay a "fee" upfront.
  • Impersonation: Someone pretending to be a bank official and asking you to update KYC (Know Your Customer) details or download a remote access app.

How to protect yourself:

  • Never share your PIN, OTP, CVV, or full card number with anyone.
  • Banks or government bodies will never ask for your PIN or OTP over the phone or message.
  • Be suspicious of unsolicited calls or messages promising large sums of money or asking for immediate payment.
  • Check the sender's details carefully for messages and emails.
  • If you receive a suspicious call or message, verify it by calling your bank's official customer care number, not the one given by the caller.
  • Report any suspicious activity or if you fall victim to fraud immediately to your bank and the police.
