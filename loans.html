Understanding Loans & Credit

Article

A loan is money borrowed from a bank or financial institution that you agree to pay back over a period of time, usually with interest . Loans can help you meet various needs like buying land, starting a business, educating your children, or dealing with emergencies.

Types of Loans:

  • Agricultural Loans: For farming needs like buying seeds, fertilizers, or machinery.
  • Home Loans: To buy or build a house.
  • Education Loans: To fund higher education.
  • Personal Loans: For general expenses, typically unsecured (without collateral).
  • Gold Loans: Taking a loan against your gold ornaments.

When taking a loan, consider the interest rate (the cost of borrowing money), the repayment period , and any processing fees . Always read the loan agreement carefully and understand all the terms before signing.

Credit History: This is a record of how well you've managed borrowed money in the past. A good credit history (paying EMIs on time) helps you get loans easily in the future at better interest rates. A poor credit history can make it difficult to borrow money. Responsible borrowing and timely repayment are key to building good credit.
